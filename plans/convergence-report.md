### Distributed-systems & scalability: federation/exchange + control/data-
- **[HIGH]** The confidential read hot-path is governed by TWO distinct consistency stores that are never reconciled, and the security-reason 'zero allow-window' contradicts the lease-TTL hot path. §4.4 defines the hot-path check as 'lease.valid && now < lease.expiry && grant not in local tombstone-since-lease' — a LOCAL LEASE read with a TTL of up to 2s (§14.5: 'Confidential allow-window = lease-TTL (2s default)'). But §4.5 states security/consent-withdrawal/compromise/GDPR-Art-7(3)/FERPA revocations get a 'synchronous deny-correct path at the owning node, zero allow-window, regardless of tier.' A lease with a 2s TTL CANNOT provide a zero allow-window unless the security-reason path bypasses the lease entirely and synchronously reads the durable tombstone log on every confidential read. The plan never says it does, and if it did, the '15ms local lease read, not a consensus round trip' latency budget (§4.3) would no longer describe the security-sensitive path. Separately, §4.3's latency table routes the owner-side authorization to a 'SpiceDB Check (local replica read)' — a THIRD store (eventually-consistent grant tuples) that also gates the same allow/deny decision alongside the lease and the durable tombstone. Which of the three is authoritative for a given decision, and how they compose, is undefined.
  - *Why:* Revocation correctness is the single property D5 exists to guarantee. If the hot path can serve a security-revoked artifact for up to the lease TTL (2s) because the deny only takes effect at lease expiry, the plan violates its own §4.5 'zero allow-window' commitment and its threat-model row R2. Conversely if every confidential read synchronously hits the durable log to honor zero-window, the published 15ms p99 and the 6.5s composite SLO are wrong. This is exactly the class of ambiguity that produces a fail-OPEN in production. It is independent of D5 (D5 says authority is local and fail-closed; it does not resolve lease-vs-log composition).
  - *Fix:* Specify the composition explicitly: (a) every confidential read does a local read of the durable tombstone high-water-mark (it is in the same cell-local Postgres, single-digit-ms, not a consensus hop) so revocation is observed immediately and the lease becomes a positive-grant cache only; OR (b) keep the lease but issue security-reason revocations as a synchronous lease-invalidation broadcast within the cell BEFORE serving any further read, making the lease the authority but with TTL=0 semantics for the security class. State which, and update §4.3's latency budget and §14.5's allow-window row to match. Also collapse the SpiceDB-local-replica Check, the lease, and the tombstone into one stated decision order with one authoritative store for the deny dimension (the durable log) and the others as narrow-only caches — the §7.3 'ABAC narrows-only' invariant should be generalized to 'caches narrow-only.'
- **[HIGH]** 'Epoch' is overloaded across two incompatible granularities, breaking the monotonic-applier and bounded-stale-bitmap claims. §4.3a and §4.4 define a per-CELL 'locally-authoritative, monotonic revocation epoch' (a single counter per owner cell, fenced) plus a per-cell tombstone bitmap replicated to the Exchange. §10.3a defines CDC consistency as 'CDC ordering keyed per-(tenant, record)' with each index record carrying 'grant_epoch' and the applier 'rejects lower-epoch applies' per record. A single per-cell monotonic counter and a per-(tenant,record) grant_epoch are different objects at different granularities. If the applier compares a per-record incoming value against a per-record stored value, a per-CELL counter cannot be that value (every record in the cell would share one counter, so a revoke of record A would bump the epoch and make a legitimate stale-but-valid apply of record B look 'lower-epoch' and get rejected, or vice-versa). The 'compact tombstone bitmap' (§4.3a) further implies bit-per-record indexing, which is yet another keying scheme.
  - *Why:* The monotonic-applier is the stated defense against replay/snapshot resurrection of a revoked record (R2b, Triage C6). If the epoch semantics are ambiguous, the applier either over-rejects (legitimate updates silently dropped → stale discovery index, a liveness/correctness bug) or under-rejects (a stale snapshot resurrects a revoked projection → the exact leak the design forbids). The bitmap-vs-counter ambiguity also means the 'compact epoch counter + tombstone bitmap' pushed to the Exchange (§4.3a) may not actually be compact (a per-record bitmap over 80-100M public records at N=200 is ~10-12MB pushed at ≤2s cadence, a non-trivial cross-region flow the §14.3 'tiny high-frequency push' claim glosses over).
  - *Fix:* Disambiguate with two named fields: a per-CELL fenced 'revocation_epoch' (monotonic counter, used only to version the tombstone set / bitmap snapshot and to detect a node moving backward on recovery) and a per-RECORD 'projection_version' (monotonic per (tenant,record), used by the CDC applier's lower-version-reject rule). State the bitmap's indexing scheme and its actual size at N=200, and the per-cycle replicated bytes, then re-justify the 'tiny push' / ≤2s p99 claim against that size. If the bitmap is large, switch to a delta/tombstone-log-tail replication so the ≤2s bound is defensible.
